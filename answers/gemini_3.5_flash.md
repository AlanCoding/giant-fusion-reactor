To power a spaceborne civilization of a billion people, we have to completely abandon Earth-bound fusion design paradigms. On Earth, we are obsessed with magnetic confinement constraints like tokamaks because real estate is expensive, structural mass is heavy, and vacuum has to be manufactured inside a steel pressure vessel.

In a deep-space environment like L4/L5, **vacuum is free, microgravity removes structural weight limits, and mass drivers give us millions of tons of raw shielding and structural materials.**

Instead of fighting instabilities in a highly stressed, curved toroidal reactor (tokamak) scaled to a ridiculous size, our design goes completely native to space: **The Megascale Linear Gasdynamic Mirror (GDM).**

---

## 1. The Core Architecture: The 12-Kilometer Magnetic Cylinder

We are bypassing the torus entirely for a linear system. A Gasdynamic Mirror uses a long, straight array of high-field superconducting solenoids to create a massive central magnetic bottle. The plasma is confined in this straight tube, bounded on both ends by incredibly intense magnetic "throats" (mirrors) that restrict, but intentionally allow, a steady leakage of plasma out the ends.

### Why Linear Scale Wins in Deep Space

* **Predictable Scaling:** In a linear system, power scales linearly with length ($P \propto L$). If you need more juice, you don't redesign the physics; you just append more modular superconducting segments onto the truss.
* **No Disruptions:** Tokamaks suffer from catastrophic plasma disruptions that can physically tear the machine apart. If a linear mirror loses confinement, the plasma simply shoots harmlessly out the open ends into deep space.
* **Structural Simplicity:** The entire reactor is built inside a lightweight, open titanium-aluminum space truss. With no gravity to bend it, a 12-kilometer-long straight structure is an elementary engineering task for automated space construction frames.

---

## 2. The Fuel Cycle: Catalyzed Deuterium (Cat-D)

D-T (Deuterium-Tritium) fusion is great for tiny Earth reactors because it has the lowest ignition temperature. But it requires breeding tritium using complex liquid lithium blankets. For a multi-terawatt industrial complex, lithium supply chains from Earth or asteroids would become a crippling logistical bottleneck.

Instead, we are running a **Catalyzed Deuterium (Cat-D)** cycle. We feed the reactor pure Deuterium—readily available from cosmic ice harvested by your space infrastructure.

$$6\text{D} \rightarrow 2\text{p} + 2\text{n} + 2^4\text{He} + 43.2\text{ MeV}$$

The primary D-D reactions naturally produce Tritium and Helium-3 as byproducts. We extract these byproducts from the exhaust stream and immediately reinject them into the core, where they burn completely.

* **The Benefit:** No lithium blankets, no complex breeding chemistry, and a massive reduction in long-term radioactive structural waste.

---

## 3. Power Conversion & The "First Wall" Problem

If you try to extract 20 to 30 Terawatts of thermal power through standard steam turbines, your power plant will weigh more than a small moon and the containment walls will liquefy instantly. We solve this with a two-pronged, space-optimized energy extraction strategy.

### Step 1: Direct Kinetic Energy Conversion (~60% of output)

Because the linear mirror is an open system, the high-energy charged particles (protons and alpha particles) leaking through the magnetic throats aren't a waste product—they are our primary electrical source.

The leaking plasma enters a massive, expanding magnetic nozzle at each end of the reactor. As the plasma expands, its thermal and kinetic energy is converted into a directed stream. This stream passes through an electrostatic **Direct Energy Converter** (essentially a particle accelerator run in reverse). The charged ions deposit their energy directly onto high-voltage grids, generating high-voltage DC electricity at **80% to 85% efficiency** without any moving parts or thermal cycles.

### Step 2: The Flowing Liquid Tin First Wall (~40% of output)

The remaining energy escapes as bremsstrahlung X-rays and high-energy neutrons. To stop this from melting the reactor structure, the inner wall of the 12-kilometer tube is shielded by a **centrifugally stabilized, flowing curtain of liquid tin**.

> **The Liquid Wall Insight:** You cannot crack, stress-fracture, or permanently damage a wall made of liquid metal. The tin absorbs the neutrons and X-rays, heating up to a staggering $1800\text{ K}$ ($1527^\circ\text{C}$).

This ultra-hot liquid tin is pumped out of the reactor to drive an advanced closed-loop Brayton cycle gas turbine system to capture the remaining thermal energy.

---

## 4. Radiating Waste Heat at Scale

In space, you can only cool things via radiation, which scales with the fourth power of temperature ($T^4$). Because our liquid tin loop operates at $1800\text{ K}$, our waste heat is rejected via an array of **Liquid Droplet Radiators (LDR)** operating at $1200\text{ K}$.

Instead of heavy structural radiator panels, the LDR shoots millions of microscopic droplets of liquid tin through the vacuum of space parallel to the reactor, where they cool rapidly via direct radiation before being captured by a collector scoop at the other end. This eliminates thousands of tons of piping mass.
