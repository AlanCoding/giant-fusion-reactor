# Linear Reactor Image Prompt

Generate a technical reactor illustration of the current linear consensus design.

## Core subject

A very large straight fusion reactor channel, viewed as a clean cutaway or isometric technical illustration.
The machine should look modular, engineered, and physically plausible, not like generic science fiction.

## Geometry to show

- A long straight central plasma tube.
- End treatment at both ends, suitable for mirror/FRC-like axial confinement and direct conversion.
- Modular sections repeated along the length.
- A clear distinction between the main burn region and the end regions.

## The three coil categories

Show these three coil systems clearly and separately:

1. Main axial coils
   - Large, simple, mostly circular or solenoidal coils.
   - Their job is to provide the bulk axial guide field.
   - These should read as the dominant structural magnets.

2. Helical or quasi-helical shaping coils
   - A secondary coil system that adds controlled twist or field shaping.
   - These should look more delicate than the main coils but still manufacturable.
   - Their purpose is to supply the transport-shaping component, not to define the whole machine.

3. Trim / correction coils
   - Smaller distributed coils placed periodically along the tube.
   - Their purpose is local correction, error compensation, and fine tuning.
   - These should look like a modular correction layer, not the primary confinement structure.

## Visual emphasis

- Make the straight bulk geometry the obvious primary structure.
- Make the twist/shaping visibly secondary, local, and modular.
- Show the coil hierarchy clearly so the viewer can tell which coils do the heavy lifting.
- Include plasma inside the tube as a glowing centralized column.
- Suggest end exhaust or direct-conversion regions without making them the visual focus.

## Style

- Clean technical concept art.
- Neutral or dark industrial background.
- Precise, readable, engineering-focused composition.
- No torus, no tokamak donut, no spherical tokamak.
- No whimsical ornamentation.
- No extreme fantasy lighting.

## Optional labeling

If labels are supported, annotate:

- main axial coils
- helical shaping coils
- trim coils
- plasma channel
- end treatment / direct conversion

## What to avoid

- A closed torus.
- A generic stellarator coil maze.
- A simple uniform tube with no shaping coils.
- Overly complex coil spaghetti that hides the architecture.
- Any impression that the reactor is just a tokamak in disguise.
